FAQ

QUESTIONS
ANSWERED.

The things most people ask before they book a survey. If you don't see your question, just call.

It depends on the size, the system specified, and whether the deck needs replacing. As a rough guide, a standard garage flat roof (around 15–20m²) typically costs £900–£1,600 for a quality Danosa torch-on system. A house extension flat roof (30–50m²) is usually £1,800–£3,500. We always give a fixed written quote before any work starts — no surprises.

Most flat roof jobs (garage, extension, bay window) are completed in a single day. A full pitched roof re-tile on a semi-detached house typically takes 2–4 days depending on size and complexity. We'll give you a realistic timeframe in the quote and stick to it.

Not necessarily. For most roofing work we don't need access inside your property. As long as we can access the roof safely, you can leave us to get on with it. We'll always call when we arrive and again when we're done. If we do need internal access (e.g. to check the loft), we'll let you know in advance.

All our workmanship carries a 10-year written guarantee. For Danosa torch-on felt systems, the materials themselves carry a 20–30 year BBA certificate. You'll receive a written completion certificate and guarantee document on the day the job finishes — not posted weeks later.

We aim to survey within 48 hours of your call, often sooner. For active leaks or storm damage we try to get someone out the same day or next morning. The survey is completely free with no obligation — we'll inspect the roof, explain what we find, and give you a written quote within 24 hours.

No — and this is important. We use Danosa BBA-certified systems for all flat roofing. These are the same materials specified by architects on new builds, not trade-counter budget felt. For pitched roofing we use reputable tile manufacturers and stainless steel fixings throughout. We'd rather charge a fair price for a job done right than cut corners and have to come back.

Yes. We carry £5 million public liability insurance and £1 million employers' liability insurance. We Are Roofing UK Ltd is registered at Companies House (No. 11861185) and is VAT registered (No. 319362107). Copies of our insurance certificates are available on request.

In most cases, yes. We carry stocks of common concrete interlocking tiles and can source clay and natural slate through our suppliers. For older or more unusual tiles we'll advise you honestly — sometimes a reclaimed tile from a salvage yard is the best match, and we can help source those too. We'll always show you a sample before ordering.

Yes — where scaffold is required it's included in our quote. We work with trusted local scaffold contractors and manage the whole process. You won't need to source or coordinate scaffolding separately. If a job can be done safely by ladder (e.g. a garage flat roof or single-storey fascias), we'll say so upfront to keep costs down.
